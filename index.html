About Avocados

Secret Life of Avocados

Avocados aren't your typical fruit. Unlike their vibrantly colored cousins, avocados boast a subdued, almost mysterious exterior. But beneath their rough skin lies a creamy, nutrient-rich flesh that has taken the culinary world by storm.

From Pit to Plate: The Journey of an Avocado

The avocado tree, native to Central America, is believed to have been cultivated for over 8,000 years. Aztecs called them "ahuacatl," which translates to "testicle tree" due to their shape. Today, avocados are a global phenomenon, enjoyed for their versatility and health benefits.
